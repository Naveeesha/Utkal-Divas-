About Odisha

Odisha, formerly known as Orissa, is a land of ancient heritage, magnificent temples, and rich cultural traditions. Utkal Divas, celebrated on April 1st, marks the formation of Odisha as a separate state in 1936. From the grand architecture of Konark Sun Temple to the divine Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha is a treasure trove of history and spirituality. The state is also known for its classical dance form, Odissi, and its vibrant festivals like Rath Yatra.

History of Odisha

Odisha's history dates back to ancient times, being home to the powerful Kalinga Empire. The famous Kalinga War fought by Emperor Ashoka in 261 BCE led to his transformation and the spread of Buddhism. Over the centuries, Odisha has been ruled by dynasties such as the Satavahanas, Guptas, and Gajapatis, each leaving a significant impact on its culture and heritage.

Cultural Capital

Bhubaneswar is known as the "Temple City of India" with over 700 temples.

Ancient Heritage

Odisha's history dates back to the prehistoric period with evidence of human settlements from 20,000 years ago.

Natural Beauty

Odisha has a 485 km long coastline along the Bay of Bengal, featuring pristine beaches.

Art Forms

Odisha is home to unique art forms including Pattachitra, Pipli work, and silver filigree.

Cultural Heritage

Odisha is renowned for its unique cultural identity, reflected in its traditional dance forms like Odissi, the oldest classical dance in India. The state is also known for its exquisite handicrafts, including Pattachitra paintings, stone carvings, and silver filigree work. The Rath Yatra of Lord Jagannath in Puri is one of the most significant religious festivals celebrated worldwide.

Modern Odisha

Today, Odisha is known for its industrial growth, rich natural resources, and strong contributions to India's economy. The state is also a hub for education and research, housing institutions like IIT Bhubaneswar, NIT Rourkela, and AIIMS Bhubaneswar. With its rich past and promising future, Odisha continues to shine on the cultural and economic map of India.
